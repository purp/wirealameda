Benefits for the community

As an anchor institution in Alameda, the public school district is an integral part of the fabric of Alameda's community. Availability of gigabit internet to homes will enable the district to increase its level of community engagement:

  • Streaming of school events. Parents and other members of the community often wish to attend school events such as graduations, sports meets, recitals, and elementary school assemblies, but cannot, due to schedule or other constraints. With gigabit connectivity, live and recorded high-definition streaming of all these events becomes possible, making them accessible to a much broader section of the community.

  • Improved access to district staff. High definition online conferencing could provide more convenient ways for parents to interact with district staff, such as their student's teachers.

  • Streaming, interactive school board meetings. AUSD's school board meets twice and month and routinely discusses issues that are of direct interest to many Alamedans - but physically attending the school board meetings is impractical for many. The board meetings are currently broadcast on the local Comcast cable TV system, but many Alamedans do not use Comcast's service. With gigabit connectivity, board meetings could be streamed online, making them available to a much higher number of Alamedans, and enabling online participants to post comments or even address the meeting in real time via the internet.

  • Adult School. Many members of Alameda's community participate in AUSD's Adult School. As students in the adult school, community residents will benefit from many of the same enhancements as children in our schools, such as increased access to classes and online materials, and distance learning.
