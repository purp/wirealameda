AUSD Board of Education Resolution 10-0035, March 9th 2010

Alameda Unified School District's Board of Education passed Resolution 10-0035 on March 9th, directing Superintendent Kirsten Vital to file a supporting RFI from AUSD, on behalf of the City of Alameda:


ALAMEDA UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Alameda, California

March 9th, 2010

Resolution 10-0035

WHEREAS, the City of Alameda is submitting an application to Google, Inc. to participate in Google's Fiber for Communities trial, and would benefit from supporting applications from significant community stakeholders;

WHEREAS, the Board acknowledges the role of the Alameda Unified School District as a significant stakeholder in the community of Alameda;

WHEREAS, the Board recognizes the educational benefits and opportunities that gigabit internet connections would afford Alameda students;

WHEREAS, the Board recognizes the benefits through competition that an additional network provider would help drive, and the operational efficiencies and improvements that gigabit internet connections could enable in the District's education programs and operations;

NOW, THEREFORE the Board hereby directs the Superintendent to submit a supporting application to Google, expressing support for the City of Alameda's application, and describing the benefits Alameda students can expect from Alameda's successful deployment of gigabit internet connectivity.

PASSED AND ADOPTED by the following vote this 9th day of March 2010.
