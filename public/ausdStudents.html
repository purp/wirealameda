Benefits for students

Google Fiber will deliver gigabit internet speeds to island homes and anchor institutions, enabling high definition video streaming, interactive video-conferencing, and much faster exchange of data over the internet. There are many education scenarios that will benefit from this:

  • Online homework clubs. Homeworks clubs are popular in our district, offering students support and encouragement in completing their homework. They typically require physical attendance on site after school hours, and per-site volunteers who are available to help. For many students - and several school sites - that's not practical. Online clubs using high-definition video conferencing address both these issues, allowing wider participation both for students looking for help, and for students, staff, and parents looking to contribute their support.

  • Cross-site access to specialized classes. Due to budget realities, specialized courses such as College Advanced Placement (AP) classes are often only available at a single school site within AUSD, and with limited availability. With high-speed internet, lessons can be streamed in real time between sites, allowing access and participation to a much wider group of students.

  • Access to recorded lesson materials from home. Recording lessons in high definition is relatively cheap nowadays, thanks to widely available high-definition cameras. With cloud-based storage and high-speed internet connections, students can access and review recorded lessons quickly online, to help them with completing their homework, doing revision, or catching up on materials missed through absence.

  • Distance learning. With high-speed connections, access to resources from other educational institutions, such as leading research institutions, universities, community colleges, etc., becomes much easier. Our students can more easily leverage educational resources sourced anywhere in the world, not just those delivered locally in Alameda.

  • Streaming of school events. With gigabit connectivity, live, high-definition streaming of school events such as graduations, sports meets, assemblies, etc., becomes possible. While primarily enabling better engagement with the commmunity, this also offers opportunities for students to participate in the production and delivery of these events, and learn valuable technical and production skills while doing so.

  • Collaboration and sharing. Opportunities for online collaboration and data sharing are limited today, because local connections are slow. With local gigabit connectivity, students can easily interact and share data with others schools in the district, and externally with other institutions.
