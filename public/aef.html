... supporting Alameda's bid for Google's Fiber for Communities

  AEF wants Google Fiber!

The Alameda Education Foundation believes that every child in the Alameda Unified School District deserves a well-rounded, high-quality education. A vital force in AUSD for more than 25 years, we raise funds to provide programs such as visual and performing arts, sports, AP classes, and scholarships to schools across the district. Looking ahead, we plan on helping the district implement both innovations in technology and new 'green' facilities and programs.

In common with our partner, Alameda Unified School District, we believe that Google Fiber can have a significant impact on educational programs in Alameda, helping to further our goal of ensuring a high-quality education for every child in the district. It can benefit AEF's specific programs in very direct ways:

  • As a charitable foundation, AEF depends entirely on donations to carry on its work. Our ability to do good for Alameda's students is tied directly to our ability to market the value of the work we do to the Alameda community and to regional donors. High definition video streaming of programs and events within AUSD will greatly increase our ability to engage our donor community, and demonstrate why they should donate to support our students.

  • Through online conferencing and collaboration, we can broaden access to our after-school enrichment programs, offering certain classes to larger numbers of students distributed across multiple school sites.

  • High definition streaming will help raise the profile of our middle school sports programs within the Alameda community, which in turn may allow us to increase their scope. The programs, which we implemented when state funding for middle schools sports was cut, offer vital opportunities for Alameda students to learn sportsmanship, goal-setting, and discipline.

We're proud of the results that AEF accomplishes within Alameda Unified School District, and we're looking forward to the benefits that Google Fiber can bring to Alameda students.



Bill Sonneman
President
Alameda Education Foundation
